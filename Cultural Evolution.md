## Overview

**Cultural evolution** is the study of how **beliefs, behaviors, technologies, and institutions change over time** through processes analogous to biological evolution â€” **variation, selection, and inheritance** â€” but operating through **learning, imitation, and communication** rather than genes.

It offers a unifying framework for understanding how **societies adapt (or fail to)** in response to changing environments, and how **organizations can participate in or steer such evolutionary processes**.

## Key Principles

### 1. Dual Inheritance Theory
Humans inherit information through **two channels**:
- **Genetic inheritance:** biological traits
- **Cultural inheritance:** socially learned traits (language, norms, tools, ideas)

These two systems coevolve â€” for example, lactose tolerance evolved **because** of dairy farming culture.

---

### 2. Variation, Selection, and Transmission
- **Variation:** People and groups generate new ideas and practices (innovation, error, creativity).  
- **Selection:** Some ideas spread more widely because they are more *useful*, *appealing*, or *fit* with context.  
- **Transmission:** Cultural traits persist through imitation, teaching, and institutions.

> In an organization like *Life Itself*, this might mean creating the right conditions for idea variation (experimentation), fair selection (feedback), and effective transmission (documentation and onboarding).

---

### 3. Cultural Group Selection
Groups that cooperate more effectively or coordinate around beneficial norms often **outcompete others**, leading to the spread of pro-social traits.

This provides a framework for understanding the **evolution of cooperation, trust, and collective purpose** â€” key elements in community-based and mission-driven organizations.

---

### 4. Cumulative Culture
Unlike other species, humans build on the innovations of previous generations â€” leading to **cumulative culture** or the *â€œratchet effectâ€*.

Organizations can cultivate cumulative culture by:
- Preserving institutional memory
- Rewarding knowledge sharing
- Iterating on existing prototypes rather than starting from scratch

## Evolutionary Dynamics in Organizations

Cultural evolution helps explain:
- Why certain **organizational practices persist** even when outdated
- How **innovation ecosystems** emerge and spread
- How **memes** and **narratives** shape collective behavior

### Examples of evolutionary dynamics:
| Level | Example | Mechanism |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Individual | A team member tries a new facilitation method | Variation |
| Group | The team adopts it after seeing results | Selection |
| Organization | The method becomes part of training | Transmission |
| Ecosystem | Other orgs adopt the same approach | Diffusion |
